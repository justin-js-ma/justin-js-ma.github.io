Justin Ma About Research

Research

Exploring the intersection of liquid crystal engineering, chemistry, and physics, I design and process advanced composite materials through techniques such as drop-on-demand printing and laser writing. My research spans the development of reconfigurable optoelectronic, microwave, and millimeter-wave devices—leveraging material design, processing, and characterization workflows. The ultimate goal is to realize high-performance, adaptive systems including wearable, flexible, and functional electronics for next-generation applications.

Overview

My research aims to bridge the gap between functional materials and real-world smart electronics by combining molecular-level LC engineering with scalable fabrication strategies. Through an interdisciplinary approach, I investigate how tailored composite structures and controllable processing environments lead to tunable performance across broad frequency and optical domains. Emphasis is placed on compatibility with modern display and communication infrastructures.

Focus Areas

Liquid-Crystal Devices

Polarization control, tunable filters, and phase shifters with wide operating ranges.

Optoelectronics

Smart windows and image-integrated LC systems with low-power drive.

Microwave & mmWave

LC-based BPF/phase shifters near 10–30+ GHz; system integration for arrays.

Printing Technology

Drop-on-Demand patterning and laser writing from millimeters to microns for functional materials.

Recent Highlights

  • Printed electrochromic PDLC smart windows with controllable image integration.
  • LC-based tunable RF components: narrow/wideband filters, phase shifters.
  • Hybrid patterning workflows for reliable LC device fabrication.
  • Demonstrated voltage-programmable color and transmittance modulation by incorporating ionic dopants into chiral nematic liquid crystals, enabling turbulence-enhanced electro-optic behavior.
  • Integrated polarization rotators and optically active layers for devices exhibiting simultaneous control over polarization, color, and light transmittance.
  • Developed ferroelectric-doped LC composites with enhanced dielectric tunability for next-generation reconfigurable smart materials.
  • Applied LC-based filters and phase shifters to phased-array systems and reconfigurable intelligent surfaces (RIS), targeting lightweight and adaptive 6G wireless platforms.
  • Established a molecular design strategy using polymeric, chiral, and doped LC mixtures optimized for both microwave (10–30+ GHz) and optical performance.
  • Utilized advanced fabrication processes including drop-on-demand printing and laser writing to enable high-resolution patterning of functional materials from millimeter to micron scale.
